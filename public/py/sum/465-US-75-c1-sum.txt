In Union & Planters' Bank v. Memphis, 189 U.S. 71, 75 (1903), this Court held that a federal court "can accord [a state judgment] no greater efficacy" than would the judgment-rendering State.
The Court is thus justified in this case to rule that preclusion must be determined under state law, even if there would be preclusion under federal standards.
