Although the "broad view" may have the merit of simplicity, this court is persuaded by the dicta in Skelly Oil Co. v. Phillips Co., supra, 339 U.S. at 671- 674, 70 S.Ct.
This court does not believe that Congress, in enacting the Declaratory Judgment Act, intended to permit a person to invoke the jurisdiction of the federal courts merely because he commenced a declaratory judgment action before the other party was able to commence his nonremovable coercive action in state court.
